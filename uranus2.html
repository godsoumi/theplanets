URANUS

Uranus has a rocky core that is surrounded by a layer of ice, with an outer envelope of hydrogen and helium. The mantle is believed to be composed of icy compounds, while the atmosphere consists of hydrogen, helium, and methane. The exact composition and internal structure of Uranus are still not fully understood due to its distance and lack of direct exploration.
